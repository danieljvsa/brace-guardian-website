MS Center


History

The beginning of the MS Center's goes back to 1957, when Professor L. van Bogaert founded the "Belgian Study Group for Multiple Sclerosis" within the Belgian Neurological Association. This project was undertaken to provide a consultation service for people with MS and to stimulate scientific research on this topic.

As it developed, Dr. Ch. J. Ketelaer, in charge, realized that it would be important to have a center that could hospitalize and treat patients, something thatwas possible after a collaboration with the study group of the Belgian Multiple Sclerosis League and a request accepted by the Ministry of Public Health. With this, in 1960 the first specialized hospital for the diagnosis, treatment and rehabilitation of people with MS and related diseases came into being.

This hospital was named "National Schiftings- en Readaptatiecentrum voor Multiple Sclerosis vzw" (National Screening and Readaptation Center for Multiple Sclerosis), however, in 1996 its name was changed to "National Multiple Sclerosis Center vzw", abbreviated to NMSC.

Mission and vision

“The National MS Centre provides comprehensive, safe, specialist clinical and integrated care plans for people with MS and other chronic neurological disorders.”
The MS Center is committed to 4 very important aspects:

  • Comprehensive approach - as medical, therapeutic, and nursing staff oversee all stages of the care process that the person with MS may go through;
  • Safe - it is committed to providing a safe, high-quality environment in all its processes;
  • World-class clinic - the MS Center has over 20 years of clinical scientific research and its medical and therapeutic care policies are based on it;
  • Integrated customised care plans - MS symptoms chenge from person to person, so a patient-focused approach is very important, so the MS Center builds on that approach to understand what care is most appropriate to provide each patient.

For more information, visit the official MS Center website: https://mscenter.be/en
